The Sex Pistols were formed in London in 1975 and quickly became one of the most influential bands in the emerging punk rock movement. The original lineup consisted of Johnny Rotten on vocals, Steve Jones on guitar, Paul Cook on drums, and Glen Matlock on bass. Managed by Malcolm McLaren, the band set out to disrupt the mainstream music scene with their raw, aggressive sound and rebellious attitude. They quickly gained a following in London's underground music scene.
